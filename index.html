Ladder

Born in a library, raised on the Semantic Web.

A revolutionary way to aggregate, enhance, and integrate your metadata, Ladder unites collections of any size into Linked Data and the Semantic Web. Designed to meet the needs of libraries, archives, and museums, Ladder enables comprehensive description and discovery for the next generation of information specialists. Fast, scalable, and user-friendly, and deployed in the cloud.

  • Describe & Discover

    Search and edit in a single system instantly. Whether you're managing physical or digital items, you see what your users see. No more batch loading or downtime to synchronize with your ILS. Discovery, rediscovered.

  • Linked Data

    Enhance and extend your information within the Semantic Web using W3C's Resource Description Framework (RDF). Aggregate and reuse data from others. Publish and share your local expertise. Get ready for Web 3.0.

  • Integrate & Unify

    Combine and organize data from multiple systems in real-time. Import messy data from any kind of XML, de-duplicate and normalize. Search all your collections at once. Say goodbye to data silos.

  • Dynamic Data

    Build and maintain relationships between resources, or add structure to existing data. Compare individual updates, or revert to an earlier change in the history. Focus on curating with less effort.

  • Standards-Friendly

    Designed around web standards and best practices. Work with library and archive standards like MARC, MODS, and EAD; or RDF vocabularies like FOAF, SKOS, and Dublin Core. Or add your own at any time.

  • Web-Scale

    Built with cutting-edge sharding technology and hosted using elastic cloud infrastructure. So whether you have a collection of five thousand or five million items, all you have to think about is your data.

  • Responsive

    A single, dynamic interface across mobile and desktop devices. See more on a larger screen and just enough on a smaller one. No need to download and install apps or clients, just take your collections with you anywhere.
